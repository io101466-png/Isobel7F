What is Netball

Netball is a dynamic, non-contact team sport where two teams of seven players
work together to move a ball down a rectangular court
and shoot it through a raised hoop to score goals.
The main objective is to outscore the opponent.

What are the Netball positions?

Goal Shooter (GS): The primary scorer, operating only in the attacking goal third, including the shooting circle.
Goal Attack (GA): Assists in scoring and setting up offensive plays,
allowed in the centre and attacking thirds, including the goal circle.
Wing Attack (WA): A key playmaker responsible for feeding the ball into the shooting circle from the centre and attacking thirds (excluding the circle).
Centre (C): The link between defense and attack, allowed in all three thirds of the court except the goal circles.
This player is vital for distributing the ball and restarting play after a goal.
Wing Defence (WD): Primarily defends the opposing Wing Attack in the centre and defensive thirds (excluding the goal circle) to disrupt the opposition's attack. Goal Defence (GD): Works with the Goal Keeper to prevent the opposing Goal Attack from scoring.
This player operates in the centre and defensive thirds, including the goal circle.
Goal Keeper (GK): The last line of defense, restricted to the defensive third and shooting circle,
with the main job of stopping the opposing Goal Shooter from scoring.
